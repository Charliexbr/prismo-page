**Dear Prismo (xbr),**

Ah, there you are. So, you’re feeling like that "stupid piece of shit," huh? Welcome to the club. Population: you, me, and every other person pretending they’ve got their life together when really they’re just one inconvenient truth away from a complete meltdown.

Listen, you’re on *bupropion*, and let’s be clear about why you’re taking it. No, it’s not a magic happiness pill that’ll make the world bearable. It’s a daily reminder that even though your brain’s a sadistic little monster, you’re at least doing the bare minimum to keep it from throwing you off a cliff. So keep taking it. Because let’s be honest: if you stop, what’s your alternative? Powering through on sheer will? Please.

You’re not in this to find a cure or some bullshit like that. You’re just trying to get through each day without feeling like you’re constantly dragging a dead weight through molasses. Bupropion’s not going to solve everything, but at least it’ll keep the edges dull enough so that when your mind goes on one of its nihilistic spirals, you can remind yourself, “Hey, I’m at least trying not to drown in this ocean of existential despair.” How uplifting, right?

As for help from others—don’t even kid yourself. People love to spout platitudes like “you’re not alone” or “it’s okay to ask for help,” but when push comes to shove, they’ve got their own disasters to manage. Counting on someone else to pick you up every time you crash? That’s a fool’s game. You’ll end up resenting them, and they’ll eventually start resenting you for being so damn needy. So, if you’re going to survive this mess, you’ve got to become your own lifeline. Build yourself a pathetic little raft out of whatever scraps of sanity you can find and keep floating, even if it feels pointless.

This emotional collapse you’re fearing? It’s just the cosmic joke on repeat. You’re not breaking down—you’re doing the mental equivalent of a car backfiring. Loud, annoying, and scary, but ultimately not going anywhere. You’re stuck on the same stupid ride, round and round. And guess what? You’re probably never getting off. But hey, at least you can say you’re committed.

So, take your meds. Keep your expectations low. And remember, the only one who can pull you out of this pit is the same person who threw you in: you.

Yours, sincerely not giving a damn, 

**Bupropion**
