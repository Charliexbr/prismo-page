<br />

It's hard to imagine all these messed-up people like me who hate themselves but still crave love from others. I thought I loved you, but let's be real—it was just laziness. I used our so-called love to distract myself from my deep self-loathing and emptiness. Did it work? For a while, sure. But here I am, still drowning in my own life, and guess what? You’re drowning too. Doubts start to flood in: Do you really love me? Do we even love each other? Or are we just two sad sacks comforting each other to catch a breath in this sea of misery? I don’t know. All I know is I hate myself. How can you love me if I can’t stand being me? The version of me you love is the one I hate. And how can I say I love you when I suddenly realize all this shit?

  *If a person loves another person only and is indifferent to the world, this is pseudo-love, a form of enlarged egotism. In this light, love is not constituted by the object, but by the faculty.*

It’s so sad to realize we may never have the kind of relationship we always wanted.
