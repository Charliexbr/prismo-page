- Running In Circlesbr<br /><br />
When asked about the new single by the Real Rock 97.7 QLZ radio, the band said “‘Running In Circles’ explores the paranoid voices inside our heads. It’s about the struggle of being real with yourself – avoiding the ‘fake’. Sometimes, it feels impossible to avoid, It was written about not being true to yourself and the shame that follows.”
---
- HURT<br /><br />
When asked about HURT, the band said “There’s not a lot in life that’s more gut-wrenchingly frustrating than trying desperately for years to build a career and having little to show for it. From living on floors and friends’ couches or out of your car, to working job after job just barely making ends meet, to going on tour and coming back with less money than you had before. We wrote this song as a symbol of that nagging doubt and insecurity. The doubt that consumes you and that can be triggered by people or events in your life, but more often than not, again it’s the voice inside your head.”
Ones to Watch described this as displaying “the group’s lyrical prowess as they explore the sacrifice required for chasing their dream and the anxiety ridden self-doubt that results”.
---
- How Could I Love You?<br /><br />
Upon dropping the double single “How could I love you? / I hope you hate me.”, the band spoke to Spinefarm Records, who wrote “Both songs address the sometimes bittersweet, more often simply bitter, fallout from a tumultuous relationship. How Could I Love You?, specifically, takes us through "an excruciating yet intoxicating journey” of the relationship until its demise.
---
- I Hope You Hate Me.<br /><br />
Like its sister single, “How could I love you?”, “I hope you hate me.” addresses the “sometimes bittersweet, more often simply bitter, fallout from a tumultuous relationship.”, this time tackling the aftermath of the relationship. The video clip, released for the song, perfectly reflects the tension of the emotional repercussions of ending something toxic, with the guitar and drums mirroring your own tense heartbeat. (From Spinefarm Records)
