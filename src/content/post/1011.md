<br />


You wake up in the morning and, of course, the first thing you do is grab your phone because heaven forbid you start the day without that sweet, sweet screen time. You scroll through whatever platform happens to catch your eye first, because why bother with real-life thoughts when you’ve got an endless stream of content curated just for you?

Swipe, swipe, swipe, it’s the usual dance. You’re not even sure what you’re looking at anymore, but hey, if it’s on your screen, it must be important. Thirty minutes go by and now it’s time for your morning video marathon. Watch a clip here, another one there. One more won’t hurt, right? Each one’s supposed to be just a few minutes long, but you blink and suddenly you’re knee-deep in an ocean of content, none of which you’ll remember by lunchtime.

You finally peel yourself off the couch, but don’t worry—the screen follows you. Background noise blares as you half-listen, half-scroll through more endless updates. You call it “multitasking,” but we all know it’s just a desperate attempt to avoid even a second of boredom or, God forbid, introspection.

Work hours blur into more screen time because, let’s be honest, it’s easier to pretend you’re engaged with life when there’s a never-ending buffet of distractions at your fingertips. And when the workday’s over? Oh, goodie—time to really dive in. You’ve got an endless array of shows and movies lined up, so why not make an event out of it? Start one, get halfway through, then switch to another, because nothing says “relaxation” like constantly needing something new to watch.

Hours slip away in a haze of autoplay and algorithmic recommendations. You tell yourself you’ll stop after just one more episode, one more video, but we both know that’s a lie. This cycle never really ends, does it? You’re a perpetual guest at the infinite content buffet, filling up on junk that leaves you starving for something real.

By the time you finally shut it all down for the night, you’ve spent your whole day lost in a blur of screens, your mind buzzing with bits and pieces of other people’s lives and stories, but not a single moment truly your own. And tomorrow? Well, tomorrow you’ll get up and do it all over again, because apparently, this is living now.
