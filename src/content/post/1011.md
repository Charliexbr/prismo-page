title: No Surf 
description: Stop spending life on the net.
dateFormatted: Sept 21th, 2024
---
<br />


Oh, wow, you wake up and grab your phone because God forbid you spend five seconds alone with your thoughts. First things first, let's check those notifications. Oh, look, three texts! You’re so popular. Then, why not dive into your email? That junk mail isn’t gonna read itself.

Next up, you know what sounds fulfilling? Scrolling through photos of other people’s curated lives. Like, like, like. “Lol” in the comments, because you’re nothing if not an empathetic genius.

Now that you’ve filled the void for a solid two minutes, let’s switch gears. Time to open that browser and get a dose of news, because staying “informed” is your top priority, right? Oh, look, another scandal you can pretend to care about for five seconds. Scroll through some sports scores, check Reddit for memes, and now... you’re mentally exhausted.

But don’t worry, YouTube is there for you. One video won’t hurt. Or two. Maybe three. Whatever, it’s “educational” content, so it doesn’t count, right?

Then, back to Messenger. Crickets. Great, so everyone else is busy with their lives, but you’re stuck on this endless loop of digital dependency. Let's hit email again. Respond to those important offers from that Nigerian prince. 

Texts again? Why not! Keep up the illusion of connection. But oh, look at the time! An hour’s gone by and you haven’t even left the bed yet. What’s next? How about some background noise to drown out the existential dread? Spotify, take the wheel.

And off to work you go, where you pretend to be productive but check your phone every ten minutes because, hey, you’re an integral part of the global economy. Emails ping, you respond like a Pavlovian dog, and YouTube or a podcast keeps you company because who needs silence?

Then it’s back home for some quality time with every streaming service you’re still paying for. Netflix, YouTube, HBO, Disney+, and more. Feel that satisfaction slipping away? Better hop online and scroll some more. 

A little gaming to wrap things up because what’s more relaxing than blowing off steam in a virtual world while your real life is in free fall?

Finally, set that alarm, because you’ve got to wake up and repeat this masterclass in life management tomorrow, and the next day, and the day after that, until you’re old, wrinkled, and wondering where all that time went.

But hey, at least you stayed “connected,” right?
